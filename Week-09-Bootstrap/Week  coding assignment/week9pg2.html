Adventure locations
What to see while at:

The Mojave National Preserve
Hart Mine
 Town ruins comprised of a crumbling chimney and a lot of disgarded relics. The townsite was originally founded when 3 prospectors found rich gold deposits in the Castle Mountains. The town ran for a few years between 1907 through 1909 during which time the town had multiple hotels, 2 general stores, a book and cigar store, real-estate office, lumberyards, 8 saloons, a candy store, post office, and a brothel. The town continued until around 1915 when the post officce closed and most of the buisness district burned down. The original mine officially shut down around 1918.
Carrara Mine
 Remains of the town of Carrara, Nv. Located a few miles from Beatty and Rhyolite. This small ghost town has the remains of a fountain some building foundations near the highway. Further into the mouontains you will find what was a marble quary. The marble from this site was used in Beatty, Rhyolite, and shipped off through out the south west but went defunct after a few years when the quality of marble became unusable.
Death Valley
 Death Valley National Park is home to multiple abandoned mine sites and ghost towns. One of the larger was a mine and townsite called Skidoo, Ca. which was in operation between 1906 through 1917. It was known for it's unique to the area 15-stamp amalgamation and cyanide mill which was operated by water. Water was pumped across the mountains and desert valley via a custom pipeline. Death Valley is also home to Badwater Basin which is the lowest point in the western hemisphere. It was named for the fact is is a giant salt flat comprised of mostly of sodium chloride (table salt), along with calcite, gypsum, and borax. Speaking of borax, this mineral was one of the cheif exports mined and transported from Death Valley.

The Great Basin National Park
Museum of the Future
 Located 13,063-feet below the summit of Wheeler Peak, The Museum of the future is actually a very old wooden structure that someone at some point posted a sign which reads "The future site of Museum of the Future."
Wheeler Peak
 Wheeler peak with it's 13,063-foot high summit is home to Great Basin National Park is home to the only glacier in Nevada, and one of the southernmost glaciers in the United States. It's peaks are home to some of the oldest Bristlecone pines ranging between 4700-5000 years old, This also makes them some of the oldest organisms in the world.

The Painted Desert
Petrified Forest
 Petrified Forest is situated in the Panted Desert region of northern Arizona. The painted desert region is known for the coloration of the hills and mountains in the area which are mostly white, reds, and purple hues. The petrified forest is the mineral remains of an ancient forest ranging around 213 million years old give or take a million years or so.
